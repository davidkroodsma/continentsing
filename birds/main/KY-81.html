These two warblers are often heard together throughout the South. First up is the Kentucky warblers chortling chuuree-chuuree-chuuree-chuuree-chuuree-chuuree-chuuree, followed immediately by the hooded warblers pure weet-weet-weet-weeto. They seem to share the airwaves, with the four songs of the Kentucky never overlapping the six of the hooded.

Background

Tufted titmouse, blue-gray gnatcatcher, white-eyed vireo, indigo bunting, prairie warbler, eastern wood-pewee, summer tanager.
