The persistent daytime zee zee zee zee zee zoo zree of a black-throated green warbler, with almost always five tonal zee notes, followed by a single low tonal zoo note, then the buzzy zree on the end. Well into the morning now he utters no chips between songs, well after hes abandoned his zree zree zoo zoo zree dawn song (see VA-61).

The persistent singing of a golden-crowned kinglet (see OR-337). And, at 1:57, every so faintly, what sounds like the single call of a Swainson's thrush!
