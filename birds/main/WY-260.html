A mountain bluebird singing 80 minutes before sunrise high above Old Faithful, which had just blown. Hes on the move, singing above me, then drifting off to the north, then swinging around and more distant, then closer again. For a good half hour he circles overhead, announcing the dawn.

Inevitably, I look for structure in what hes up to, and label the notes within his first song. I come up with this sequence:

a b c d e f g b c d e f g b

This first song suggests that he picks a favored sequence of notes (b c d e f g, underlined) and then repeats it so that he can extend his song. In other songs, he introduces a few other notes, but his repertoire of song notes seems limited to a dozen or so.

Background

The continuous hiss of Upper Geyser Basin, white-crowned sparrow.
