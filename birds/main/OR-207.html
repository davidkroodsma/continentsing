Often throughout the day, one hears these treetop songs, the bird itself rarely seen. In daytime singing, this western wood-pewee offers 21 bzeeyeer songs over three minutes, seven songs per minute. That's about 1/6th of his dawn pace, and now he sings only his bzeeyeer song--how differently he sings now compared to his grand performance before sunrise.

Background

Cedar waxwing (loud "begging" beginning at 0:43), western tanager, house wren, spotted towhee (nice songs, calls), American robin, American goldfinch (e.g., 2:54).
